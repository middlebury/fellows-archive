Yasmine Ryan

Fluency in French has helped Yasmine Ryan cover North Africa for the past five years as a journalist for Al Jazeera English. But a 2012 assignment to report on the attacks on the U.S. Embassy in Benghazi, Libya, confirmed that she needed Arabic to do her job well. “A journalist’s duty is to provide an accurate and fair account of reality and to ensure that the widest range of voices are heard,” Yasmine says. “For those of us working in international affairs, this is particularly important because our reports can have a direct impact on global decision-makers.”

Now based in Tunisia, Yasmine grew up in New Zealand and studied journalism and political science in France. She has received numerous awards for her work, including an international Award for Excellence in Journalism for a piece on Algerian migrants who flee to Europe by boat. She was also the first English-speaking reporter to cover the uprising in Tunisia in December of 2010 that would spark what would later be termed the Arab Spring.
